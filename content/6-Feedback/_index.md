### General Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The working environment is generally friendly and open. Team members at FCJ are usually willing to offer support when I encounter difficulties, even outside working hours. The environment feels comfortable, which helps me stay focused. However, due to the large number of interns, opportunities to work onsite at the office are somewhat limited, and most of the work is done remotely.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Administrators**  
Mentors have been quite supportive throughout the project, helping to answer questions and provide guidance. In addition, workshops and events are organized to share useful knowledge with us.

**3. Alignment Between Work and Academic Major**  
Before starting the internship, I did not have much experience with AWS or cloud services. During the internship, I had the opportunity to learn more about this field and gradually understand its importance in my area of study. My project, which focuses on an automated incident response and forensics system on AWS, is more aligned with Information Security. Although it is not exactly the same as my current major, it has still been a valuable learning experience.

**4. Learning Opportunities & Skill Development**  
Throughout the internship, I had the chance to develop several skills, such as working with AWS services, using Python for the project, and improving collaboration with team members, especially in a remote working environment.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The company culture is positive, with mutual respect among members and a balance between professionalism and a comfortable atmosphere. The mentors are young and approachable, making communication easier. Team members are generally proactive, responsible, and supportive, especially during the final stages of the project.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The program allows flexible working arrangements and supports remote work. Additionally, the company introduces AWS Study Groups and organizes knowledge-sharing sessions, which help provide useful learning directions.

### Additional Questions

- **What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
  **Answer:** I had the opportunity to learn how to work with AWS services and better understand how to properly configure policies when building systems.

- **What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  
  **Answer:** Having more workspace available at the office would be beneficial, although I understand it may be difficult to implement. Another point is the midterm assessment, which had a broader scope than we initially expected. If the scope had been communicated more clearly in advance, we could have prepared better.

- **Would you recommend this internship to your friends? Why or why not?**  
  **Answer:** I would consider recommending it, but it may be more suitable for those who already have a well-organized team and can maintain discipline when working remotely. Some OJT interns might face challenges when being assigned to randomly formed teams.
